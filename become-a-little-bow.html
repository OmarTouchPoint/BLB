A Little Bow at Big Little Bows is a brave soul embarking on a journey of self-discovery and healing. These are remarkable LGBTQ+ teens, aged 15-17. Each Little Bow is a beacon of resilience, seeking guidance as they navigate the complexities of their identity and the road to recovery. Through our program, they find not just support, but a family—a community of understanding, empathy, and unwavering encouragement.

Matched with a Big Bow mentor who shares their journey and identity, they embark on a path of growth, empowerment, and hope. At Big Little Bows, we cherish and nurture the potential within every Little Bow, believing wholeheartedly in their ability to triumph over adversity and emerge as radiant, empowered individuals.

Enroll a Little Bow


If you're a teen between the ages of 15-17 and identify as LGBTQ+, you can enroll as a Little Bow at Big Little Bows. We understand that parental consent is important when working with adolescents, so please involve your parents or guardians in the enrollment process

Enrollment Process:

Parental Input

Parents, if you believe that your child could benefit from the guidance and support of a Big Bow mentor, please reach out to us at contact@biglittlebows.org. We're here to answer any questions you may have and to discuss how our program can support your child's journey to sobriety and mental wellness.

Contact Us

Once you have parental consent, ask your parents to contact us at contact@biglittlebows.org to express your interest in enrolling as a Little Bow. Our team will guide you through the enrollment process and provide you with the necessary information and support.

Matching Process

After enrolling, your child will be matched with a supportive and understanding Big Bow who shares their gender identity, expression, and orientation. Your child's Big Bow mentor will provide personalized guidance, support, and encouragement throughout their journey.

Access Support

As a Little Bow, your child will have access to educational workshops, resources, and tools for lasting sobriety and mental wellness. They'll also become part of a supportive community of LGBTQ+ teens who understand and empathize with their experiences.

Big Bow Mentor Qualifications

Parents, rest assured that our Big Bows undergo a thorough vetting process to ensure the safety and well-being of our Little Bows. Each Big Bow applicant is required to:

  • Undergo a Background Check: We conduct comprehensive background checks to ensure the safety of our program participants.
  • Submit a Written Application: Big Bows are required to complete a detailed written application outlining their qualifications and commitment to our mission.
  • Participate in an In-Person Interview: Applicants undergo a thorough in-person interview to assess their suitability as mentors for our Little Bows.

If you're ready to enroll your child as a Little Bow or if you have any questions about our program, please don't hesitate to reach out to us. We're here to help you every step of the way.

Becoming a Big Bow

Are you passionate about making a difference in the lives of LGBTQ+ teens struggling with substance use disorder and mental health challenges? Consider becoming a Big Bow and joining our team of dedicated mentors. As a Big Bow, you will have the opportunity to provide guidance, support, and encouragement to a young person in need.

Qualifications:
  • Personal experience overcoming challenges with substances, gender identity, and being a proud member of the LGBTQ+ Community
  • Minimum of two (2) years of sobriety
  • Compassionate and empathetic attitude
  • Ability to actively listen and provide non-judgmental support
  • Commitment to the well-being and success of our Little Bows
