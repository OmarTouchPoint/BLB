About Joey Bachrach

Joey Bachrach is the visionary founder of Big Little Bows, dedicated to bringing hope and support to LGBTQ+ teens struggling with substance use disorder and mental health challenges. His own journey through addiction during his teenage years inspired him to create a program where young individuals could find the guidance and support he wished he had during his own struggles.

From Teenage Addiction to Visionary Leader

Joey's journey with addiction began during his teenage years when he struggled with alcoholism. He faced these challenges without the guidance or support of someone who truly understood what he was going through, particularly as an out teen.
Determined to make a difference and provide the support he lacked, Joey embarked on a mission to create a community where LGBTQ+ teens could find understanding, empathy, and mentorship.

The Birth of Rainbow Hill

In 2021, Joey, alongside his husband, Andrew Fox, brought their vision to life with the inception of Rainbow Hill.

This organization has since become a beacon of hope for members of the LGBTQ+ community, providing a safe and affirming space for individuals to heal and thrive in sobriety.

Inspiring the Creation of Big Little Bows

During the creation of Rainbow Hill, Joey's vision for Big Little Bows began to take shape.

Recognizing the need for targeted support for LGBTQ+ teens struggling with substance use disorder and mental health challenges, Joey was inspired to launch Big Little Bows as an extension of his commitment to supporting individuals on their journey to recovery and wellness.

Bringing Hope and Light to Others

Joey is excited to expand Big Little Bows' reach, bringing hope and light to individuals across the country who need a little extra support and encouragement.

He firmly believes that recovery is possible for everyone, and through programs like Big Little Bows, we can empower individuals to achieve their goals and live fulfilling lives.

Join Joey Bachrach and the Big Little Bows team in our mission to provide support, guidance, and hope to LGBTQ+ teens in need.

Together, we can make a difference and show that recovery is possible for everyone.

Becoming a Big Bow

Are you passionate about making a difference in the lives of LGBTQ+ teens struggling with substance use disorder and mental health challenges? Consider becoming a Big Bow and joining our team of dedicated mentors. As a Big Bow, you will have the opportunity to provide guidance, support, and encouragement to a young person in need.

Qualifications:
  • Personal experience overcoming challenges with substances, gender identity, and being a proud member of the LGBTQ+ Community
  • Minimum of two (2) years of sobriety
  • Compassionate and empathetic attitude
  • Ability to actively listen and provide non-judgmental support
  • Commitment to the well-being and success of our Little Bows
